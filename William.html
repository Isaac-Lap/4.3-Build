William Llyod Garrison

Born: December 10, 1805
Primarily worked as: journalist and editor
His main cause: Abolishing slavery

Biography

William Lloyd Garrison was a prominent abolitionist who mostly worked to end slavery through his newspaper called "The Liberator," which he founded and edited, advocating for the immediate abolition of slavery. He also co-founded the American Anti-Slavery Society.

Notable contributions

  • 1840 -Refused to sit in the main hall of the World Anti-Slavery Convention because women were not allowed in. Instead, in protest, he sat in the back room with the women.
  • mid 1830s-Refused to participate in government saying that the Constitution was a pro-slavery document and to participate at all was to support it.
  • mid 1830s encouraged women to start their own organizations against slavery if they had to.

Famous quotes

" I Will Not equivocate; I will not excuse; I will not retreat a single inch - AND I WILL BE HEARD"
